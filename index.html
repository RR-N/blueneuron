Kevin Wayne Rusch


I'm a Cuban-born, German-bred, US-based computational cognitive neuroscientist with backgrounds in biochemistry and computational modeling.

I'm currently working as a Bioinformatician (II) doing computational neuroscience at the University of Massachusetts' Yemini Lab, where I'm working on using transformer-based architectures to facilitate neuron-level detection and tracking in C. Elegans. I also work as a Research Project Manager at the Kording Lab at the University of Pennsylvania. Hired under the CENTER grant, most of my work there revolves around metascience and specifically rigor in scientific research.

Beyond that, I also serve on the executive committee of Neuromatch Academy, I run the biggest online community of neuroscience researchers, students, and enthusiasts, and I've been a Science & Tech contributor for some non-profit outlets.
